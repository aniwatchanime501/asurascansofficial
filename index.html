What is Manhwa? Complete Guide to Korean Comics & Webtoons

Manhwa (ÎßåÌôî) is the Korean term for comics and graphic novels, similar to Japanese manga but with distinct storytelling styles and cultural influences. Unlike traditional manga, manhwa often features more realistic art styles, modern urban settings, and focuses heavily on character development and emotional depth.

Manhwa vs Webtoon vs Manga: Key Differences

Korean Manhwa

  • Read left-to-right (like English)
  • Color illustrations common
  • Realistic art styles
  • Modern, urban settings
  • Strong character development

Korean Webtoons

  • Vertical scrolling format
  • Optimized for mobile reading
  • Full-color digital art
  • Interactive elements possible
  • Weekly serialization

Japanese Manga

  • Read right-to-left
  • Black and white art
  • Diverse art styles
  • Various settings and genres
  • Traditional panel layout
